Understand what the users need.

We conducted a in-depth interview with 11 candidates, including 2 fine-art learners, 2 architects, 5 amateurs and 2 teachers (graduated in fine art major). Also, another 5 tours with art school were taken to understand what the potential users need.

  • What is the importance of drawing ability for you?

  • What is your biggest difficulty in learning painting?

  • What kind of guidance do you most want to get?

  • Are you interested in AI painting teachers?

    / The Discover

    The learner expectations changed over time.

    I was surprised by the issues we found. The students learner expectations changed over time. They felt like to have an enjoyable learning experience to keep their passion, rather than a boring and difficult study journey to learn more techniques.

    Most of respondents believe that online education would be more attractive and convenient than the traditional course. The AI teacher can have a definite advantage on systemic knowledge and data-based analysis, comparing to human beings.

    “...I hope that AI can teach me more fundamental and systematic skills, inspire my imagination instead of the boring lecture video only... ”

    The feedback shows that the users do not care much about the accuracy of AI ​​evaluation. They would like to take the result as a reference for their future learning.

    Useful information scattered

    Skill level unknown

    Target audience not focused

    Learning direction unclear

    Difficult to keep practicing

    We summarized the interview results and found the following insights:

  • Insight #1: Useful information scattered – It is hard for students to find out the information, such as art courses, workshops, exhibitions, etc.

  • Insight #2: Skill level unknown - Students often feel confused about their status and do not know exactly what the skill level they currently have.

  • Insight #3: Target audience not focused - The course content is usually the same for all levels of students, ignoring the difference in each person's ability and characteristics.

  • Insight #4: Learning direction unclear - The students may know what they pained is not good enough, but they still have no idea which is the best way to modify.

  • Insight #5: Difficult to keep practicing - The improvement of drawing ability cannot complete overnight, and it is really a big challenge for art learners to keep practicing always.
